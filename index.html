Doctor of Philosophy - PhD, Bioinformatics

G protein-coupled receptors (GPCRs) are integral membrane proteins that transmit signals embodied in the chemical structure of endogenous and synthetic agonists from outside to inside the cell. Thus, they are fundamental in physiological and pathological conditions. GPCRs are flexible entities able to adopt multiple conformations which may activate different signaling pathways. Membrane environment affects the agonist-induced receptor activation process. Allosteric interactions exerted by lipids affect the population distribution of active and inactive receptor states, thus leading to a complex signaling network. In order to provide answers to the relation of GPCR structure and function with human diseases, molecular dynamics (MD) has become a successful computational approach for obtaining information regarding the protein conformational landscape and interactions with ligands and other macromolecules.
